# Average Annual Family Income in 2023 is Estimated at PhP 353.23 Thousand

## TL;DR (One Sentence)
Filipino family income rose 15% to PhP 353.23k in 2023, while expenditure grew 12.8% to PhP 258.05k.

## Core Problem & Motivation
No specific research gap—this is a national statistical report. It tracks household economic well‑being through income and expenditure trends across regions, enabling policy comparisons.

## Methods / Approach (Bulleted)
- Data source: Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) Family Income and Expenditure Survey (FIES).
- Survey years: 2018, 2021, and preliminary 2023.
- Estimates cover national and regional averages (in thousand PhP).
- Percent change computed between 2018–2021 and 2021–2023.

## Key Findings / Results (Numbered or Bulleted)
1. National average family income in 2023: **PhP 353.23 thousand** (↑15.0% from 2021).
2. National average family expenditure in 2023: **PhP 258.05 thousand** (↑12.8% from 2021).
3. Highest income region: NCR – **PhP 513.52k** (↑22.9% from 2021).
4. Lowest income region: BARMM – **PhP 206.88k** (↑9.7% from 2021).
5. Highest expenditure region: NCR – **PhP 385.05k** (↑19.7%).
6. Largest % income increase: SOCCSKSARGEN – **20.2%** (2021→2023).
7. Largest % expenditure increase: Zamboanga Peninsula – **20.8%**.
8. All regions saw expenditure growth; CAR had smallest increase (2.1%).

## Important Figures & Tables (Condensed)
- Table 1 (Income by region): Shows NCR (513.52) → BARMM (206.88); the gap widened 2021–2023 as NCR grew faster (22.9% vs. 9.7%).
- Table 2 (Expenditure by region): NCR highest (385.05), BARMM lowest (168.91); Zamboanga Peninsula led growth at 20.8%.
- Figure 1: Bar chart comparing national income and expenditure (2018, 2021, 2023) – income and expenditure both increased from 2021 lows.

## Limitations & Assumptions
- 2023 estimates are **preliminary** and may change.
- Regional boundaries changed (e.g., Negros Island Region established, BARMM gained 63 barangays in 2023), affecting comparability.
- Inflation not adjusted – nominal figures only.
- Survey excludes institutional populations.

## Takeaway for Memory (≤ 3 bullet points)
- 🔑 **Income rose 15%, spending 13%** – families earned **PhP 353k**, spent **PhP 258k** in 2023.
- 📌 **NCR earns double BARMM** – PhP 514k vs. PhP 207k; income gap persists.
- 💡 **Fastest income growth in SOCCSKSARGEN (+20%)** – rural regions catching up but still far below NCR.
