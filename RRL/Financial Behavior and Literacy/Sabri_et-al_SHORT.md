# Impact of financial behaviour on financial well-being: evidence among young adults in Malaysia

## TL;DR (One Sentence)
Financial behaviour fully mediates the effects of literacy, socialisation, self‑control, and fintech on young Malaysians’ financial well‑being.

## Core Problem & Motivation
COVID‑19 reduced income and savings for young adults (18‑29) in Malaysia, yet prior research was mostly descriptive. The study tests whether financial behaviour is the key mechanism linking four predictors to financial well‑being.

## Methods / Approach (Bulleted)
- **Sample**: 360 young adults (18‑29) from five regions via multi‑stage random sampling.
- **Constructs**: Financial literacy, financial socialisation, self‑control, financial technology (fintech), financial behaviour, financial well‑being (FWB).
- **Analysis**: Structural equation modelling (SEM) with bootstrapping; Sobel‑Goodman test for robustness.
- **Model fit**: RMSEA=0.046, CFI=0.964, χ²/df=1.749 (all good).

## Key Findings / Results (Numbered or Bulleted)
1. Financial behaviour explains **74%** of variance in FWB (R²=0.74).
2. Financial behaviour **fully mediates** the relationship between all four predictors and FWB (p<0.05 for all indirect effects).
3. Direct effect of fintech on FWB: **not significant** (β= -0.035, p=0.674).
4. Direct effect of self‑control on FWB: **not significant** (β=0.158, p=0.069).
5. Direct effect of financial literacy on FWB: **not significant** (β=0.096, p=0.068).
6. Only financial socialisation had a **significant direct effect** on FWB (β=0.31, p=0.003).
7. Fintech is the strongest mediator (proportion mediated = **58.8%**), followed by literacy (48.1%) and self‑control (52.0%).

## Important Figures & Tables (Condensed)
- **Figure 1 (Research model)** → Predictors → Financial behaviour → Financial well‑being.
- **Table 9 (Bootstrapping)** → Indirect effects all significant; direct effects non‑significant except socialisation.

None. (Mediation is path‑based: indirect effect = a×b, tested via bootstrapping.)

## Critical Citations
- [Xiao & Porto, 2017] – Financial behaviour as mediator between literacy and well‑being.
- [Sabri et al., 2022] – Positive financial behaviour improves FWB in low‑income young Malaysians.
- [Achtziger et al., 2015] – Poor self‑control leads to excessive debt.

## Limitations & Assumptions
- Sample only ages 18‑29 (not generalisable to older adults).
- Multi‑stage random sampling but only one state per region.
- Subjective measure of FWB (no objective metrics like savings amount).
- Did not examine socio‑economic backgrounds or childhood experiences.

## Takeaway for Memory (≤ 3 bullet points)
- 🔑 **Knowledge alone isn’t enough** – financial behaviour is the engine; fintech helps only if you act responsibly.
- 📌 **Fintech mediates 59%** – using apps without good behaviour does not raise well‑being.
- ⚠️ **Self‑control has no direct effect** – only works through better financial habits (like budgeting).
