## I--Machireddy_2023

### Description

Explains how advanced machine learning models and real-time data streams can be utilized for financial vulnerability assessment to identify early warning signs of personal financial risk, which addresses the limitation of lacking predictive and anomaly detection capabilities in existing apps.
- Behavioral Profiling Integration: Notes that some personal finance applications attempt to measure user mood and incorporate contextual and psychological information to customize guidance, which relates to the absence of behavioral user profiling as a limitation in other applications.
- Forecasting Financial Outcomes: Discusses using machine learning models to classify or forecast financial outcomes, such as predicting customers likely to be unable to pay a credit card bill or forecasting the probability of loan default, indicating the necessity of predictive features that are often absent in simpler budgeting tools.

## I--Parameswaran_&_Saad_2025

### Description

Identifies a limitation in existing mobile applications as lacking the necessary intelligence to provide personalized financial guidance and relying on generic financial advice that fails to account for individual spending patterns and goals.
- Generic Recommendations: Points out that current personal finance applications often treat financial management with a "one-size-fits-all approach," which results in recommendations that are irrelevant or counterproductive to users' actual financial situations.
- Limited Analysis and Anomaly Detection: Notes that a significant shortcoming in existing personal finance applications is their failure to differentiate between fixed and variable expenses and their inability to comprehensively analyze spending patterns or detect spending anomalies.

## I--Singh_2025

### Description

Explains that conventional financial decision-making systems rely on static and generalized models, such as demographic information, which often fail to address the highly contextual and real-time needs of modern users, contrasting this with Digital Persona Modeling (DPM).
- Lack of Behavioral Profiling: Proposes Digital Persona Modeling (DPM) as a method to capture dynamic behavioral, contextual, and intent-driven attributes, suggesting existing applications lack this kind of sophisticated behavioral user profiling.
- Absence of Context-Awareness: States that the DPM framework is designed to enable context-aware financial decisioning, which is implicitly positioned as an improvement over existing applications that lack this adaptive capability.

## I--Sipila_2025

### Description

Identifies usability, lack of features, and low demand as the most significant limitations facing Personal Financial Management (PFM) applications, according to a review of relevant literature.
- Manual Processes and Data Trust: Notes that a stakeholder's existing financial tracking system relied on fragmented tools and manual processes, representing challenges also identified in the literature, such as repetitive manual updates and limited trust in data accuracy.
- Desire for Automation: Highlights that young users preferred PFM functionalities that featured minimal manual input and automated categorization features, indicating a desire to avoid manual transaction entry.
