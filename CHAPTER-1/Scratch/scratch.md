# Definition of Terms

1. Financial Literacy is a dimension in making financial decisions. According to Rodriguez et al. (2024), financial literacy directly affects expenditure through financial behaviors. The better their financial knowledge, the better spending decisions are made whole adopting positive financial habits like budgeting and saving. In this study, we will use financial literacy as measure on how Filipino young professionals will utilize informal financial mechanisms in managing their finances.

3. Paluwagan is an informal group saving or money-lending system in the Philippines built and relying on trust and commitment among participants (Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas, 2021). This study analyzes how group spending habits in paluwagan affects the financial behavior of Filipino young professionals.

7. Financial Behavioral Profile is a feature in our system that classifies users into one of four profiles (Stable-Obligated, Stable-Flexible, Varaible-Obligated, and Variable-Flexible) based on onboarding information to personalize budget guidance.
8. Spending Forecast is a feature in our system that predicts future spending based on historical data. It analyzes previous spending habits and patterns to generate predictions for future spending. The spending forecast covers the next 7 days.
9. Anomaly Detection is a feature in our system that detects unusual spending patterns. It analyzes transactions and identifies patterns that deviate from the user's normal spending habits. The user will be alerted for potential overspending or unusual spending activity.
