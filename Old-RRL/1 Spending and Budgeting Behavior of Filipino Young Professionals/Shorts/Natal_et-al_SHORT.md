# UNDERSTANDING FINANCIAL BEHAVIOR: AN ANALYSIS OF PERSONAL FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT AMONG WORKING PROFESSIONALS AMIDST THE GLOBAL INFLATION SURGE

## TL;DR (One Sentence)
Demographics (age, gender, industry, job type, income) do **not** affect saving or buying habits among young Filipino professionals during inflation.

## Core Problem & Motivation
No prior study examined how single working professionals (24–35) in Balayan, Batangas manage finances during the global inflation surge. Researchers aimed to identify saving/buying behaviors and strategies to cope with rising prices.

## Methods / Approach (Bulleted)
- Mixed‑methods: quantitative survey (N=75, 4‑point Likert) + qualitative interviews (n=5 per industry).
- Quota sampling: five industries (education, finance, government, healthcare, retail).
- Data analysis: frequency/percentage, descriptive statistics, Pearson chi‑square, thematic analysis.
- Instruments: survey on saving habits & buying behavior; semi‑structured interviews.

## Key Findings / Results (Numbered or Bulleted)
1. **No demographic factor** (age, gender, industry, employment type, income) showed significant relationship with saving habits or buying behavior (all p > 0.05).
2. Overall saving habits mean = **3.56** (Strongly Agree) – top item: “save money for future wealth” (mean 3.69).
3. Overall buying behavior mean = **3.35** (Strongly Agree) – top item: “assess practical value before buying” (mean 3.59).
4. Qualitative themes: budgeting, frugality, conscious saving, minimal impact for some.
5. Key strategies: buy in bulk, seek discounts/cheaper alternatives, fixed‑percentage saving (20% income), automatic 10% salary transfer, need‑based purchases.

## Important Figures & Tables (Condensed)
- **Table 2** (Saving habits): Highest ranked item #10 (save for future, 3.69), lowest #9 (track expenses, 3.36). Overall strongly agree.
- **Table 3** (Buying behavior): Highest #7 (assess value, 3.59), lowest #9 (reduced discretionary spending, 3.19 – only “Agree”).
- **Tables 4–13**: All chi‑square tests (age, gender, industry, employment, income vs. saving/buying) → **not significant** (p > 0.05).

## Limitations & Assumptions
- Sample limited to one municipality (Balayan) – not generalizable to all Philippines.
- Only single professionals aged 24‑35 – excludes married or older workers.
- Self‑reported survey data – potential social desirability bias.
- Cross‑sectional design – cannot infer causality between inflation and behavior changes.

## Takeaway for Memory (≤ 3 bullet points)
- 🔑 **Demographics don’t dictate** – age, income, gender, industry: none change saving/spending habits during inflation.
- 💡 **Top tactic** – assess actual value before buying (mean 3.59) and save for future (mean 3.69).
- 📌 **Universal response** – professionals across all sectors **prioritize savings and cut non‑essentials**, regardless of salary.
