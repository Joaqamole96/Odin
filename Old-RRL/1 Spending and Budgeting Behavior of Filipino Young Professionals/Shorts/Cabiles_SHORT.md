# Financial Management Practices of Employees at Bureau Of Internal Revenue

## TL;DR (One Sentence)
BIR employees budget and save well but struggle with spending discipline, investing, debt accumulation, and low interest‑rate awareness.

## Core Problem & Motivation
Employees face financial uncertainty due to weak personal finance skills. The study measures current budgeting, spending, saving, and investing practices to identify gaps and propose targeted financial literacy programs.

## Methods / Approach (Bulleted)
- Survey of **permanent employees** at BIR RDO 068, Sorsogon City.
- **Online and pen‑and‑paper** Likert‑scale questionnaire covering four domains.
- **Descriptive statistics** (frequency, weighted mean, ranking) for quantitative data.
- **Mixed‑methods embedded design** – qualitative challenges collected via open‑ended ranking.

## Key Findings / Results (Numbered or Bulleted)
1. **Budgeting** is strong (mean 4.32 for emergency funds), but digital apps underused (mean 3.07).
2. **Spending** prioritizes quality (4.10) yet expense‑goal alignment is only “sometimes” (3.50); impulse buying (3.20) and low credit card use (2.52).
3. **Saving** is the strongest domain (4.37 set aside income), but employees avoid higher‑risk saving (2.66).
4. **Investing** shows a **value‑action gap** – knowledge (3.98) ≠ action (real estate 3.51, risk‑taking 3.03).
5. **Financial literacy engagement** is low (budgeting 2.90, spending 3.27, saving 3.08–3.83, investing 3.17).
6. **Impact of literacy programs** is perceived as high (overall 4.14), but application of learned knowledge remains moderate.
7. **Top three challenges**: debt accumulation (rank 1), lack of interest‑rate awareness (rank 2), insufficient budgeting knowledge (rank 3).

## Limitations & Assumptions
- Single revenue district (RDO 068) – may not generalize to all BIR employees.
- Self‑reported data – social desirability bias possible.
- Cross‑sectional design – cannot infer causality.
- Relies on Likert‑scale frequency (“sometimes”, “frequently”) – vague cutoffs.

## Takeaway for Memory (≤ 3 bullet points)
- 🔑 **Strong saving, weak investing** – employees save regularly but fear higher‑risk options, missing growth potential.
- 💡 **Debt + interest ignorance = top problem** – rank 1 and 2 challenges; focus workshops on debt literacy and interest rates.
- 📌 **Knowledge does not equal action** – VAG appears in investing (know options but rarely invest) and literacy application (high perceived impact, low daily use).
