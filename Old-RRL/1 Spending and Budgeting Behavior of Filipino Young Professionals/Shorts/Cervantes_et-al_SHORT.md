# The Effect of Online Buying Decision on Personal Budget of Business Administration Students

## TL;DR (One Sentence)
Online buying decisions have a strong positive relationship with poor personal budgeting among Filipino business students, driven by social media, instant gratification, and marketing tactics.

## Core Problem & Motivation
Students often overspend due to online shopping, but little is known about how specific factors (social media, impulsive tendencies, promotions) affect their personal budgets. The study fills this gap by examining business administration students in a Philippine university.

## Methods / Approach (Bulleted)
- Descriptive-correlational design with 153 randomly selected business students.
- Validated questionnaire covering demographics, online buying behavior, and budgeting practices.
- Used SPSS for Pearson’s r (correlation) and ANOVA (group differences).
- Four‑point Likert scale (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree).

## Key Findings / Results (Numbered or Bulleted)
1. Most respondents are female (62%), aged 18‑24 (83%), with monthly allowance ≤ ₱1,000 (77%).
2. Online buying decisions are rated “High” (mean 3.02 / 4), driven by likes/comments (3.10) and social media platforms (3.05).
3. Personal budgeting is rated “High” (mean 3.15), with necessary expenses rated “Very High” (3.32).
4. **Correlation is strong and significant** (r = 0.612, p < 0.05) – more online buying leads to worse budget control.
5. Age and civil status significantly affect allowance, spending, and savings; sex affects only savings.

## Important Figures & Tables (Condensed)
- Table 4: Correlation matrix → **r=0.612** (strong positive relationship) [core evidence].
- Table 5: Demographic differences → Age and civil status reject null for all three financial variables; monthly allowance affects only savings.

## Critical Citations
- [Davenport & Batra, 2018] – Established that online shopping features (discounts, ease) cause spontaneous purchases.
- [Johnson & Williams, 2017] – Showed that promotions and website design lead to budget challenges, supporting the need for financial literacy tools.
- [Delos Santos & Reyes, 2023] – Philippine study confirming that targeted ads and one‑click buying disrupt monthly budgets.

## Relevance to Odin

**Pillar(s):**
Pillar 1: Spending and Budgeting Behavior of Filipino Young Professionals, Pillar 4: Budgeting Strategies and Budget Recommendation in Personal Finance Systems, Pillar 7: User Behavioral Profiling in Filipino Personal Finance Contexts

**How it contributes:**
This paper provides empirical evidence that online buying decisions (influenced by social media, instant gratification, and marketing) negatively affect personal budgeting among young Filipino students. It directly justifies Odin’s focus on behavioral triggers (likes, flash sales, peer influence) as features that must be countered through budgeting recommendations. The demographic findings (age, allowance level) support tailored profiling for Filipino users.

**Directly justifies:**
- The need for real‑time spending alerts when users engage with social‑media promotions (Pillar 4).
- Inclusion of “impulse buying tendency” as a behavioral feature in user profiling (Pillar 7).
- Budgeting strategies that account for low‑allowance users (₱1,000/month) who are most vulnerable to online overspending (Pillar 1).
- The use of financial literacy nudges within e‑commerce interfaces to reduce unplanned purchases (Pillar 4).

**Limitations of relevance:**
- Sample limited to business students in one Philippine campus, not young professionals (though overlapping demographics).
- No predictive model or algorithm tested; only correlational, so does not directly justify predictive pillars (5, 6).
- Published in 2025 – recent but not yet widely cited; use as primary evidence rather than meta‑analysis.

## Limitations & Assumptions
- Single institution, N=153 – may not generalize to all Filipino students.
- Self‑reported data (questionnaire) subject to social desirability bias.
- Assumes four‑point Likert scale is interval; no validation of equidistance.
- No control for prior financial literacy levels.

## Takeaway for Memory (≤ 3 bullet points)
- 🔑 **Online buying → worse budget** (r=0.61) – a strong link you cannot ignore.
- 💡 **Social media likes matter** – highest influence on purchase decisions (mean 3.10/4).
- 📌 **Age and civil status change everything** – they affect allowance, spending, and savings.
